# GcfLcm Deep Dive



GCF and LCM: Math Grade 4-5 (MATATAG)

The 
Greatest Common Factor (GCF)
 is the largest number that divides evenly into two or more numbers. The 
Least Common Multiple (LCM)
 is the smallest number that is a multiple of both numbers.

Using Prime Factorization

Step 1:
 Break each number into its prime factors (factor tree).

Step 2:
 Put the factors into a Venn diagram.

Step 3:
 The 
GCF
 is the product of the 
common
 (overlapping) factors.

Step 4:
 The 
LCM
 is the product of 
all
 factors in the entire diagram.

Example: For 12 and 18:

12 = 2 × 2 × 3

18 = 2 × 3 × 3

Common: 2 × 3 = 
GCF = 6

All: 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 = 
LCM = 36
