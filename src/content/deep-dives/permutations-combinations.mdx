# Permutations & Combinations Deep Dive

**Permutations and Combinations: Math Grade 10 (MATATAG)**

Both permutations and combinations deal with choosing items from a group, but they differ in whether **order matters**.

## Permutation (Pagkakaayos)

Order **MATTERS**. Choosing a President, VP, and Secretary from 5 people is a permutation because each role is different.

$$
P(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n - r)!}
$$

## Combination (Pagpili)

Order **DOES NOT matter**. Choosing 3 people for a committee from 5 is a combination because all committee members have the same role.

$$
C(n, r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n - r)!}
$$

Notice how permutations always produce a larger number than combinations (or equal, when $r \le 1$). That's because multiple orderings of the same group collapse into one combination!

## Dissection of Variables

- **$n$ (Total Items)**: The total number of unique items you have available to choose from.
- **$r$ (Selected Items)**: The number of items you are actually choosing or arranging.
- **$!$ (Factorial)**: Multiply a series of descending natural numbers. E.g., $5! = 5 \times 4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1 = 120$.
