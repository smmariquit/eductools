# ReactionRate Deep Dive



Reaction Rates: Science Grade 10 (MATATAG)

Chemical reactions occur when reactant particles 
collide
 with enough energy. The 
Collision Theory
 explains that not all collisions lead to reactions — only those with enough 
activation energy
 and the correct orientation.

Factors Affecting Reaction Rate

Temperature (Temperatura):
 Higher temperature → faster particles → more energetic collisions → faster reaction.

Concentration (Konsentrasyon):
 More particles → more frequent collisions → faster reaction.

Catalyst (Katalista):
 Lowers the activation energy needed, so more collisions become successful without changing the products.

Watch the blue and red reactant particles collide — when they have enough energy, they react and turn 
green
