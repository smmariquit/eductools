# Pythagorean Deep Dive



Pythagorean Theorem: Math Grade 8 (MATATAG)

The 
Pythagorean Theorem
 states that in any right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the longest side, opposite the right angle) equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Formula

a²
 + 
b²
 = 
c²

Visual Proof (Patunay)

If you build a square on each side of the triangle, the 
area
 of the two smaller squares always adds up exactly to the area of the largest square. This isn't a coincidence — it's a fundamental law of geometry!

Try a classic 3-4-5 triangle: 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5². The areas perfectly balance!
