# Projectile Motion: The Physics of Tumbang Preso

Tumbang Preso provides a perfect real-world laboratory to study 2D kinematics. The *pamato* (slipper) acts as a projectile moving under the influence of gravity, tracing a parabolic trajectory. To ensure a *sapul* (direct hit), we must mathematically dissect the variables of the throw.

## Dissection of the Variables

### 1. Anggulo / Launch Angle ($\theta$)
- **Unit**: Degrees ($^\circ$) or Radians ($\text{rad}$).
- **What it actually is**: The angle above the horizontal at which the *pamato* is released. This determines the proportion of the initial velocity dedicated to gaining height ($v_y$) versus covering ground distance ($v_x$). The maximum theoretical range in a vacuum occurs at $\theta = 45^\circ$.

### 2. Bilis ng Hagis / Initial Velocity ($v_0$)
- **Unit**: Meters per second ($\text{m/s}$).
- **What it actually is**: The speed at which the *pamato* leaves your hand. Because projectile motion is two-dimensional, we break this vector down using trigonometry:
  - **Horizontal Component**: $v_{0x} = v_0 \cos(\theta)$
  - **Vertical Component**: $v_{0y} = v_0 \sin(\theta)$

### 3. Taas at Layo / Kinematic Equations ($x, y$)
- **Unit**: Meters ($\text{m}$).
- **What it actually is**: The horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) positions of the slipper at any given time ($t$). Since gravity only pulls *downward*, the horizontal speed remains constant, while the vertical speed changes.
  - $x(t) = v_{0x} \cdot t$
  - $y(t) = v_{0y} \cdot t - \frac{1}{2}g t^2$

### 4. Oras / Time of Flight ($t$)
- **Unit**: Seconds ($\text{s}$).
- **What it actually is**: How long the slipper stays in the air. The total time until it hits the ground is determined entirely by the vertical components and gravity:
  - $t_{\text{total}} = \frac{2v_{0y}}{g}$
