# SolutionConcentration Deep Dive



Solutions and Concentration: Science Grade 7 (MATATAG)

A 
solution
 is a homogeneous mixture of a 
solute
 (the substance being dissolved) and a 
solvent
 (the substance doing the dissolving). The 
concentration
 tells us how much solute is dissolved in a given amount of solution.

Paraan ng Pagsukat ng Konsentrasyon

Percent by Mass (%m/m):
 (Mass of Solute / Total Mass of Solution) × 100. If you dissolve 10g of salt in 90g of water, the total solution mass is 100g, and the percent by mass is 10%.

Percent by Volume (%m/v):
 (Mass of Solute / Volume of Solvent) × 100. Used when mixing a solid into a liquid.

Saturated vs. Unsaturated

Every solute has a 
solubility limit
 — the maximum amount that can dissolve in a given volume at a specific temperature. NaCl (table salt) dissolves up to ~36g per 100mL of water. If you add more than that, the excess salt sinks to the bottom — the solution is 
saturated
.
